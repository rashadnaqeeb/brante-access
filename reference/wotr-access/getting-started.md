# How to Play
You play by commanding your party members to move and interact, almost as you would if playing a real-time strategy game. Let's take the introduction as an example. After you are past the intro dialogue, you are given a small amount of time to explore the Kenabres festival.
The game will start out unpaused. You can press space to pause and unpause or you can use the corresponding menu item. Use pause often to assign commands to your characters. This is a feature of the game and this style of game and the game is designed around it; pause often.
You can use the movement cursor (wasd) to explore the area. Depending on your sonar settings you will hear a number of sounds around you indicating where npcs, interactables, etc are located. When the cursor hovers over something you will hear a sound for it. Pressing enter will command your character to walk towards the unit and interact with it if they reach the target. If the wall tones are on you will hear sounds as you approach walls and other obstacles. Note that your characters will pathfind automatically towards their commanded destination; this is how the game works and not a mod feature. Alternatively you can use the scanner or quick keys (period, comma, etc) to find what you want to interact with and press i.
Pressing b will show you all points of interest for the current area. Note that points of interest are not technically interactable objects, they are just map markers. If you press slash on one, it will jump your cursor to it. Find the object near there and command your party members to move or interact.
To complete this introduction, you need to interact with 2 of the 3 festival attractions (the drinking contest, dart throwing competition, or the training dummy). Once past that you will be thrown into the first real dungeon of the game.

# The Caves Beneath Kenabres
This section will contain slight spoilers for this area. It won't contain anything very specific, but I wanted to give a walkthrough of the first few sections to give a better overview of how to play the game. If you would prefer to figure things out on your own you should be well equipped now to experiment. Don't be afraid to tinker with mod settings too; you can reset any category to its default values at any time.

You start out in a small room that is overall in the northern section of the area. Press c to ensure the cursor is at your character's location (it can sometimes end up in weird spots after an area transition; this is being worked on.) Feel free to use wasd to look around this area; note how the wall tones work and how their volume changes as you get closer to a wall.

You'll also notice this room is not a neat square/rectangle. Pathfinder is a 3d game, so rooms are realistically modeled, including curved and uneven walls. It is simplified compared to other 3d games due to the camera viewpoint, but still something to keep in mind.

When the cursor has moved far enough, you will hear a higher pitched bing noise. This indicates you have entered fog of war, aka any area your characters can't see due to distance or line of sight. Press ctrl a to select your entire party, then press backspace to move all of the selected characters closer. This will reveal the hidden areas. If your characters aren't moving for some reason, ensure the game is unpaused.

This is also a good moment to try l and shift l, which cycle unexplored space: the openings where walkable ground you haven't revealed yet borders ground you have — the places you could go to push the map back. Each press names the opening (with its room, when known) and how far away it is. Press slash to jump the cursor there, then backspace to send the party. Whenever you're unsure where to explore next, l is the answer.

There isn't anything hidden in this very starting room and there is only one way forward. Head overall to your south. The room will curve to the southwest and, once your characters are past a certain point, a scripted animation of rocks falling will occur.

You can either use the scanner here or press n to cycle neutral units to find Anevia, who is trapped under some fallen debris. Press i to interact with her. A conversation will occur; choose any options you like. You can fail the initial skill check, but it doesn't have any negative consequences as the game is being nice to you... For now. Note that pressing space on dialogue or dialogue options will allow you to view any links within that text.

After the dialogue Seela will join your party and Anevia will follow as a neutral unit. Seela can be directly commanded but Anevia is fully AI controlled. She will follow you but won't help you in combat.

You can have up to 6 party members. Note that this limit of 6 only includes specific characters; any summons, animal companions, etc don't count towards this limit. Yes this makes animal companions very powerful.

You can either command all party members at once (ctrl a) or you can select a specific party member via the ctrl 1 through ctrl 6 shortcuts. When commanding party members at once, the movement command will move all of them, but they will try to stay in a formation. You can rearrange that formation in the accessible formation window (the Formation button in the in-game menu).

Select your whole party and navigate the cursor up to the next fog of war. This hallway curves to the west and northwest. Keep approaching the fog of war and eventually you will hear a new sonar sound for a revealed container if you have it enabled. Seela will comment on it as well.

Interact with the container to loot it. You can either tab to loot all to loot everything, or just hit enter on each item. Pressing space on an item will let you review its information first. Continue to the northwest-ish to find another lootable item, Terendelev's Scale (you definitely want these.) Proceeding further northwest will lead to a cutscene and another party member, Camellia.

Before continuing, it is worth reviewing how to obtain information on your characters. You can either press ctrl c to bring up the character sheet for the selected character menu or you can use the corresponding button in the windows menu of the UI. The character sheet has a number of tabs that change parts of the character sheet. If you are confused about what changed, check the bottom sections of the charsheet. You can also level up here.

Next, open the inventory (ctrl i) for your main character. Note that they aren't equipped with any items. Equip them with what you think makes sense for your character. Keep in mind too that all items are shared; you don't have to juggle 700 items between characters  every 5 minutes as you would in other games. Seela and Camellia will both start out with some equipment, though it may be worth adjusting depending on your setup. Particularly on higher difficulties it can be worth swapping Camellia to use a ranged weapon as she tends to go down in one or two melee hits. Continue onto the north and west to face your first encounter.

# Combat
This encounter consists of two centipedes. The game will automatically pause when the first one becomes available. The second one is concealed by the fog of war to the west.

When one of your characters gets within 25 to 30 feet of an enemy, combat will start. Different enemies do have different aggro ranges, so be careful with your movement when enemies are nearby. Depending on positioning, you will be able to single out smaller enemy groups and not have to fight all of them at once. That doesn't apply here though, there is no way to avoid the second centipede joining the encounter.

There are two combat modes for Pathfinder. The first is real time with pause (rtwp). In rtwp, characters will act simultaneously in realtime as the name would suggest. You can configure the game to pause automatically when certain triggers occur (spell is cast, new enemy revealed, etc.) Rtwp is very hectic though by design.

The other mode is turn based. By pressing ctrl t you can swap between rtwp and turn based at any time (even when in combat.) Turn based mode works like it does in the tabletop Pathfinder game; characters act in initiative order (rolled at encounter start.) For newer players I recommend using turn based mode, but feel free to experiment.

Pause the game before positioning or making any combat preparations. Note that for any spellcasts/etc to go off, you will need to unpause the game and wait out the duration of the casts. Simply move at least one character in range to start the encounter, though if you would like an advantage you can have everyone use an ability first. For example, let's say your main character has the charge ability and is mounted (this will not apply to many characters, especially casters.) Note that Seela has charge but she can't use it due to being fatigued.

Tab to the action bar and select charge. The mod will tell you that you are now targeting. Press period to select the enemy you want and press i, or move the cursor over the enemy and press enter. You have now queued up a command for your main character to use charge once the game is unpaused.

Once you have queued up any abilities you would like to use, unpause the game and the battle will start.

## Combat, RTWP
If in realtime with pause, the game will most likely automatically pause when the battle starts as this is the default behavior. Unpause to continue the combat. If any abilities are queued up, your characters will execute them. Every action, including movement, takes a variable amount of time as you would expect. Various things will automatically pause the game, depending on your settings.

If a specific action isn't queued up, characters will act automatically based on the game's AI. Most of the time this is fine; it would be annoying having to pause the game, command every single standard attack, then unpause. Make sure to pause the game when you do want to queue up specific commands; this is definitely not meant to be played fully in real time.

When one of your characters moves west the second centipede will be revealed (which will probably pause your game.) It is up to you how to deal with this. The AI will probably automatically target and beat it with little  trouble, though you of course have the option of commanding a ranged character to try to take it out before it reaches any of your melee for example.

## Combat, Turn Based
In turn based mode you can't really queue up a number of actions to be executed immediately when the fight starts, since combat rounds occur in initiative order regardless. Turn based combat is much simpler than rtwp. When a command is input (attack, move, etc) it is executed immediately. Note that the pause key is skip/end turn in turn based combat. There is an additional panel in the UI as well that you can tab to, containing the turn order and some additional turn based mode actions.

# What is next?
Continue to explore this area. The path curves southwest from here. There are several more encounters along it and several optional alcoves off to the west and southwest (one of these has loot.) The way forward after that point leads east and south.

Something that may help with orientation is the rooms system. The mod uses an algorithm to mark various regions of the map as rooms. You have probably heard the game announcing the cursor is in a new room; this is what that was referring to. You can press v to cycle through room exits. Note that doors are automatically marked as room exits later on. Pressing z gives you "where am I", which includes the room your cursor is currently in; shift x speaks a hand-written description of the room, and x one of the scanner object you're focused on, where they've been authored. And if you've lost the thread of the dungeon entirely, l cycles the unexplored openings that remain.
